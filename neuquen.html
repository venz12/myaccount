Neuquén City never sleeps

Villa La Angostura

Villa La Angostura is a resort town on Nahuel Huapi Lake in Patagonia, Argentina. It’s known for its Alpine-style wooden buildings. The surrounding Nahuel Huapi National Park has Andean mountains, lakes and forests.

Nearby, Los Arrayanes National Park contains centuries-old arrayán trees. Cerro Bayo mountain ski trails are just outside of town. The Seven Lakes road winds north to the town of San Martín de los Andes.

Limay River

Throw your line in and see if you can catch a large trout at Limay River. Known in particular for its excellent fly fishing, the Patagonian river flows for 380 km (240 mi) from Nahuel Huapi Lake, until it eventually merges with Rio Negre. Set up shop near Picún Leufú to give yourself a great chance of catching rainbow and brown trout, or choose another of the river's many good fishing spots.

Ride a raft down the river, under the supervision of a guide, and learn about local cultures while admiring the nature surrounding you.

Paseo de la Costa de Neuquen

Take a stroll through Paseo de la Costa de Neuquen and admire the river and the natural beauty of the area. As you walk down the well-developed paths, take a moment to observe the locals and tourists relaxing or exercising by the river.

Swimming is forbidden here, but you can rent a kayak if you wish to go out on the water. The area also boasts several food stands and restaurants, many with outdoor seating.

Lago Huechulafquen

Sitting at the heart of a national park, Lago Huechulafquen offers plenty of accessible hiking, fishing, and relaxation--all with spectacular views of the Andes. The pristine azure glacial waters stretch across a surface area of 104 km sq (40 sq mi).

The areas around the lake have excellent walking trails as well as camping sites. Vantage points afford sweeping vistas of mountains and Lanin volcano, so bring a camera. Tour operators run excursions to the area. If you'd rather not camp, explore the lodgings available on site.
